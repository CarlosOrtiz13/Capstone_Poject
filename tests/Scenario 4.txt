In early March 2024, the security team at Meridian Financial Group, a mid-sized 
investment bank headquartered in London, detected unusual outbound traffic originating 
from several internal workstations in the trading floor network segment.

Forensic investigation revealed that an attacker had gained initial access through a 
spearphishing email sent to three employees in the finance department. The email 
contained a malicious Word document that exploited CVE-2023-21716 to execute a 
PowerShell dropper script.

The dropper downloaded a Cobalt Strike beacon from hxxps://update-service.microsoftedge-cdn[.]net/payload.bin 
and established a C2 channel to 185.220.101.47. The beacon communicated over HTTPS 
on port 443 using a malleable C2 profile to blend with legitimate traffic.

The attacker performed credential dumping using a modified version of Mimikatz, 
then moved laterally across the network using stolen administrator credentials. 
Several sensitive files related to upcoming merger activity were staged at 
C:\Users\Public\Temp\ before being exfiltrated to a secondary domain: 
secure-vault.financedocs[.]io.

The attacker also established persistence by creating a scheduled task named 
MicrosoftEdgeUpdateTaskMachineUA and dropping a backdoor DLL into 
C:\Windows\System32\edgeupdate.dll.

Attribution indicators suggest overlap with a known Eastern European financially 
motivated threat group tracked as FIN7, also known as Carbanak. Contact was made 
via the attacker-controlled email address ops.handler[@]protonmail.com.
